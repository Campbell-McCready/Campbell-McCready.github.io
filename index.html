Week 3: Using a visualisation in economic commentary

I considered an argument made at the Festival of Economics stating that Britain's export market should be improved to boost economic growth. To form my opinion on this statement I had to look at the UK's imports and exports.

We can see from the diagram above the orange import line is consistently above the blue export line, meaning the UK regularly runs a trade deficit. This is shown more clearly by looking at the net trade, the difference between imports and exports, shown below:

As we can see the green trade balance line is consistently negative, only spiking into positives in 2020 and 2021. Therefore, it can be argued that there is a strong case to boost UK exports, creating economic growth.
